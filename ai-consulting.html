What AI consulting actually is

Most "AI consulting" is a deck, a Notion doc, and a handoff. That's not consulting. That's procurement.

Real AI consulting answers three questions for your business, in order:

  1. Where does AI fit here? Which of your workflows are actually a good match for an LLM, an agent, or retrieval - and which ones are a trap.
  2. What should you build? The specific system, the right model, the right harness, the right evaluation story, the right roll-out.
  3. How do you keep it running? Monitoring, guardrails, prompt versioning, cost ceilings, failure modes. The unsexy half that decides whether your AI feature lives past launch.

An AI consultant who stops at question one is selling strategy. A good AI consultant answers all three - and can code the third themselves if needed.

When to hire an AI consultant

You probably need AI consulting if any of this is true:

  • You have an AI feature in production, and it's working but unpredictable. Some queries are great, some embarrass you. You've tried prompt fixes. They bought you a week.
  • You're about to spend six figures on an agent platform and want a second opinion from someone who doesn't get a referral fee.
  • Your team has shipped software for years but has never shipped an LLM system. You know the road is different but don't know where the potholes are.
  • You're a non-technical founder who wants to understand what you're paying for before you pay for it.
  • You have a workflow that eats 40 hours a week across your team, and you suspect AI could eat the workflow instead.
  • You want to build an AI agent, know the word "MCP," and want to skip three months of learning which architecture doesn't fall over.

How I'm different from an AI consulting firm

I am one person. That's the pitch.

  • No pyramid. The person you meet on the call is the person who thinks about your problem, writes the code, and replies to your late-night "wait, what about this edge case" Slack message.
  • No retainer for retainer's sake. If the right answer is "don't build this," I will tell you and hand you back the money for the rest of the engagement.
  • No vendor allegiance. I like Claude. I use GPT and Gemini. I recommend open source when it's right. I have no partnership checks to chase.
  • Shipped beats theorized. Every opinion I have is one I formed by doing it in production - agents that run on a schedule, MCP servers that connect to real systems, retrieval pipelines serving real users.
  • Free by default. My framework and expertise library are public and free because the fundamentals should not be paywalled. Consulting is what happens when a team needs your specific problem solved, not a general read.

How engagements work

  1. Intro call (30 min, free). You describe the problem. I tell you honestly whether AI consulting is the right answer, whether I'm the right person, and what a sensible next step looks like.
  2. Scoping. If we move forward, I write a one-page proposal with deliverables, timeline, and a fixed fee. No open-ended billing.
  3. Engagement. Strategy engagements produce a decision document plus a reference architecture. Build engagements produce working code in your repo with tests and a runbook.
  4. Handoff. Your team walks away with something they can maintain. That's the whole job.

What you walk away with

Depending on scope, a typical engagement delivers some combination of:

  • A written strategy doc naming which workflows to automate, in what order, with which technology.
  • A working proof-of-concept agent or MCP server in your repo, committed, tested, deployed.
  • A production evaluation suite so you know whether your AI system is getting better or worse.
  • A playbook for your team covering prompts, guardrails, monitoring, cost controls, and incident response.
  • A direct line for the next 30-90 days so your team is not alone when something breaks at 2 AM.

AI consulting vs. hiring a full-time AI engineer

Different tools for different jobs:

Situation Right move
You have one hard problem, no AI team, want it solved in weeks. AI consultant
AI is core to the product, you will ship features monthly for years. Full-time hire
You have an AI team but nobody senior; direction is drifting. Advisory consultant
You want a discovery pass before committing to a hire or vendor. Consultant, short engagement
You need 40 hours a week of AI engineering for 12+ months. Hire. Do not retain a consultant.

Frequently asked questions

What is AI consulting?

AI consulting is advisory and implementation work that helps a team figure out where AI fits in their business, what to build, how to build it, and how to keep it running. Good AI consulting produces working systems, not slide decks.

How much does AI consulting cost?

Engagements range from a single paid discovery session (a few thousand dollars) to multi-week build engagements (five figures and up). The right shape depends on whether you want strategy, architecture review, or hands-on implementation. Reach out and I will quote the specific problem.

Do you build agents or just advise?

Both. Advisory work is short and outputs direction. Build engagements produce working agents, MCP servers, Claude Code harnesses, or retrieval pipelines running in your environment.

What tech stack do you work with?

Claude and the Anthropic API, Claude Code, MCP servers, agent frameworks (LangGraph, Claude Agent SDK, CrewAI), vector stores, evaluation tooling, and whatever glue the specific problem needs.

Who is this for?

Founders, operators, and engineering leaders at seed-to-series-B companies shipping AI features, plus established businesses exploring where generative AI fits without burning a year of budget learning in public.

Will you sign an NDA?

Yes, happy to. Most engagements start with one.

Do you work on-site?

Remote by default. On-site for a kickoff or a stuck-point intensive, case-by-case.

How do I know if AI is even the right answer?

That's literally question one of the intro call. If the honest answer is "automation with no AI would be faster and cheaper," I will tell you that. It's saved clients more than AI ever has.
