About

I'm Samuel. I study and write about autonomous AI, how to build agents that actually work, how to push systems toward real autonomy, and where AI is in its life cycle as a tool.

The short version

Serial entrepreneur turned independent AI researcher. Business degree (Farmingdale State College, 2019, 3.9 GPA). New York-based. Spent the last several years building, advising, and learning at the intersection of product and AI.

These days I focus on two things: writing the framework you see on this site, and advising people quietly when asked.

What I believe about AI

Models are commodities. Products aren't.

The interesting frontier isn't the weights, it's the product layer. How do you get a language model to reliably do a job? How do you build an agent that survives contact with real users? How do you ship something that actually makes something better?

That's what I think about. That's what I write about. I'm not chasing AGI. I'm chasing applied reliability.

Why this site

The best AI writing today is scattered, papers, blog posts, Twitter threads, official docs. Useful, but nobody is writing the integrated handbook for someone who wants to go from zero to autonomous.

This site is my attempt. Forty-plus pages on Claude, MCP, plugins, Claude Code, agent patterns, and going fully autonomous. No hype. No secrets. Just what I wish I'd read when I started.
