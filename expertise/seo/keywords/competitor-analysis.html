Competitor keyword analysis

📖 3 min readUpdated 2026-04-18

Competitor keyword research shortcuts 80% of the manual work. Instead of generating keywords from scratch, you pull your competitors' top-ranking pages and use them as your seed list.

Who counts as a competitor for SEO?

Not necessarily your business competitors. SEO competitors are any sites that show up on the same SERPs for queries you care about. This includes:

  • Direct competitors (same product/service)
  • Indirect competitors (same audience, different product)
  • Content-only competitors (blogs, review sites, media)

How to identify SEO competitors

  1. Search your top 10 priority keywords
  2. Note which domains appear in the top 10 across multiple queries
  3. Those are your real SEO competitors

This frequently reveals competitors you hadn't considered (affiliate sites, industry blogs, trade publications).

The content gap analysis

Every major SEO tool has a "keyword gap" or "content gap" feature. You enter 2-4 competitor domains; the tool returns every keyword they rank for that you don't. That's your gap, your target list.

Variations:

  • They rank, you don't: immediate opportunity
  • They rank top-10, you rank 20-50: push existing content up
  • They rank, you rank but lower: their content strategy is working; study it

Which competitor pages to study closely

For each competitor, pull their top-20 pages by traffic. Look for:

  • Which pages drive the most traffic (their wins)
  • Which keywords those pages rank for
  • What format those pages are (guide, listicle, tool, data study)
  • How many backlinks each has (authority signal)

The reverse-engineering question

For each of your competitor's top pages, ask: "Could I produce something better? What's my angle?"

"Better" means at least one of: more depth, more recent data, original research, unique case studies, clearer structure, better UX, or coverage of a sub-topic they missed.

Watch out

  • Don't clone. Imitation without improvement rarely beats the incumbent.
  • Check if their pages still rank. Some "competitors" ride legacy authority even though their content is old.
  • Some competitor keywords won't fit your business. Filter for relevance before prioritizing.
